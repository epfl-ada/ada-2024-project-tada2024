Matilda I or Maud (1105– 3 May  1152), was  Queen consort of the  Kingdom of England, the wife of King Stephen. She was also sovereign  Countess of Boulogne.She was born in  Boulogne, France. She was the daughter of  Eustace III, Count of Boulogne and his wife Princess Mary of Scotland, herself the daughter of King Malcolm III of Scotland and  Saint Margaret of Scotland. In 1119 Matilda married Stephen, Count of Mortain, later King of England, and proved his strongest supporter during the period of civil war known as the Anarchy. Stephen and Matilda had three sons:
 Eustace IV of BoulogneBaldwin (d. before 1135) William of Blois, Count of Mortain and Boulogne and  Earl of Surrey.
They also had two daughters, Matilda and  Marie of Boulogne.Around 1125, her father died and she succeeded as Countess of Boulogne. She ruled this area jointly with her husband until 1150, when she reigned alone until 1151, when the County was given to her eldest son Eustace, then her surviving son William inherited it, and then her daughter Marie.Matilda died at  Hedingham Castle,  Essex, England and is buried at  Faversham Abbey, England.
